Its exploration of themes such as love, pride, and redemption, along with Mahendran's nuanced direction, ensures that "Mullum Malarum" remains a timeless and influential film that continues to resonate with audiences.

Visaranai

"Visaranai" is a hard-hitting Tamil crime thriller directed by Vetrimaaran, released in 2016. The film is based on true events and follows the harrowing journey of four Tamil laborers who are unjustly accused and brutally tortured by the police to confess to a crime they did not commit. Featuring powerful performances by Dinesh, Samuthirakani, Anandhi, and Aadukalam Murugadoss, "Visaranai" exposes the stark realities of police brutality and systemic corruption within the justice system. The film's raw and realistic portrayal of these issues, combined with its intense narrative and gripping direction, creates a deeply impactful viewing experience. "Visaranai" received critical acclaim for its unflinching storytelling and was honored with multiple awards, including the Amnesty International Italia Award at the Venice Film Festival. Its unvarnished depiction of human rights abuses and the quest for justice resonates strongly with audiences, making "Visaranai" a significant and unforgettable film in Indian cinema.

Iruvar

"Iruvar" is a Tamil political drama directed by Mani Ratnam, released in 1997. The film offers a fictionalized account of the complex relationship between two iconic figures in Tamil Nadu politics, M. G. Ramachandran (MGR) and M. Karunanidhi. Starring Mohanlal as Anandan, loosely based on MGR, and Prakash Raj as Tamizhselvan, inspired by Karunanidhi, "Iruvar" explores their rise to power, friendship, and eventual rivalry. With Aishwarya Rai in her debut role as Kalpana, a talented actress entangled in their lives, the film delves into the intricacies of politics, cinema, and personal ambition. Mani Ratnam's direction, along with A.R. Rahman's evocative music and Santosh Sivan's stunning cinematography, creates a visually stunning and emotionally resonant cinematic experience. "Iruvar" is celebrated for its nuanced storytelling, powerful performances, and its portrayal of the complexities of power and influence in Indian society. It remains a masterpiece that continues to be studied and appreciated for its depth and relevance in the realm of political cinema.
